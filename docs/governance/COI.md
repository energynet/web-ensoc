<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='text-align:center'><span
style='font-family:"Courier New"'>Conflict of Interest Policy and Agreement<o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><span style='font-family:"Courier New"'><o:p>&nbsp;</o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='text-align:center'><span
style='font-family:"Courier New"'>Article I - Purposes<o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><span style='font-family:"Courier New"'><o:p>&nbsp;</o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><span style='font-family:"Courier New"'>It is important for Energynet
Society directors, officers, and staff to be aware that both real and apparent
conflicts of interest or dualities of interest sometimes occur in the course of
conducting the affairs of the corporation and that the appearance of conflict
can be troublesome even if there is in fact no conflict whatsoever.<span
style='mso-spacerun:yes'>  </span>Conflicts occur because the many persons
associated with the corporation should be expected to have and do in fact
generally have multiple interests and affiliations and various positions of
responsibility within the community.<span style='mso-spacerun:yes'>  </span>In
these situations, a person will sometimes owe identical duties of loyalty to
two or more corporations.<span style='mso-spacerun:yes'>  </span>The purpose of
the conflict of interest policy is to protect the corporation’s tax-exempt
interest when it is contemplating entering into a transaction or arrangement
that might benefit the private interest of an officer or director of the
corporation or might result in a possible excess benefit transaction.<span
style='mso-spacerun:yes'>  </span>The policy is intended to supplement but not
replace any applicable state and federal laws governing conflict of interest
applicable to nonprofit and charitable organizations.<o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><span style='font-family:"Courier New"'><o:p>&nbsp;</o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><span style='font-family:"Courier New"'>Conflicts are
undesirable because they potentially or eventually place the interests of
others ahead of the corporation’s obligations to its charitable purposes and to
the public interest.<span style='mso-spacerun:yes'>  </span>Conflicts are also
undesirable because they often reflect adversely upon the person involved and
upon the institutions with which they are affiliated, regardless of the actual
facts or motivations of the parties. However, the long-range best interests of
the corporation do not require the termination of all association with persons
who may have real or apparent conflicts that are harmless to all individuals or
entities involved.<o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><span style='font-family:"Courier New"'><o:p>&nbsp;</o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><span style='font-family:"Courier New"'>Each member of the
board of directors and the staff of the corporation has a duty of loyalty to
the corporation.<span style='mso-spacerun:yes'>  </span>The duty of loyalty
generally requires a director or staff member to prefer the interests of the
corporation over the director’s/staff’s interest or the interests of
others.<span style='mso-spacerun:yes'>  </span>In addition, directors and staff
of the corporation shall avoid acts of self-dealing which may adversely affect
the tax-exempt status of the corporation or cause there to arise any sanction
or penalty by a governmental authority.<o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><span style='font-family:"Courier New"'><o:p>&nbsp;</o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='text-align:center'><span
style='font-family:"Courier New"'>Article II - Definitions<o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><span style='font-family:"Courier New"'><o:p>&nbsp;</o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><span style='font-family:"Courier New"'>Section 1. Interested
Person.<o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><span style='font-family:"Courier New"'><o:p>&nbsp;</o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><span style='font-family:"Courier New"'>Any director,
principal officer, or member of a committee with governing board delegated
powers, who has a direct or indirect financial interest, as defined below, is
an interested person.<o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><span style='font-family:"Courier New"'><o:p>&nbsp;</o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><span style='font-family:"Courier New"'>Section 2. Financial
Interest.<o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><span style='font-family:"Courier New"'><o:p>&nbsp;</o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><span style='font-family:"Courier New"'>A person has a
financial interest if the person has, directly or indirectly, thorough
business, investment, or family:<o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><span style='font-family:"Courier New"'><o:p>&nbsp;</o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoListParagraphCxSpFirst style='margin-left:.75in;mso-add-space:auto;
text-indent:-.5in;mso-list:l2 level1 lfo1'><![if !supportLists]><span
style='font-family:"Courier New";mso-fareast-font-family:"Courier New"'><span
style='mso-list:Ignore'>(a)<span style='font:7.0pt "Times New Roman"'>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
</span></span></span><![endif]><span style='font-family:"Courier New"'>An
ownership or investment interest in any entity with which the corporation has a
transaction or arrangement,<br style='mso-special-character:line-break'>
<![if !supportLineBreakNewLine]><br style='mso-special-character:line-break'>
<![endif]><o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoListParagraphCxSpMiddle style='margin-left:.75in;mso-add-space:
auto;text-indent:-.5in;mso-list:l2 level1 lfo1'><![if !supportLists]><span
style='font-family:"Courier New";mso-fareast-font-family:"Courier New"'><span
style='mso-list:Ignore'>(b)<span style='font:7.0pt "Times New Roman"'>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
</span></span></span><![endif]><span style='font-family:"Courier New"'>A
compensation arrangement with the corporation or with any entity or individual
with which the corporation has a transaction or arrangement, or<br
style='mso-special-character:line-break'>
<![if !supportLineBreakNewLine]><br style='mso-special-character:line-break'>
<![endif]><o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoListParagraphCxSpLast style='margin-left:.75in;mso-add-space:auto;
text-indent:-.5in;mso-list:l2 level1 lfo1'><![if !supportLists]><span
style='font-family:"Courier New";mso-fareast-font-family:"Courier New"'><span
style='mso-list:Ignore'>(c)<span style='font:7.0pt "Times New Roman"'>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
</span></span></span><![endif]><span style='font-family:"Courier New"'>A
potential ownership or investment interest in, or compensation arrangement
with, any entity or individual with which the corporation is negotiating a
transaction or arrangement.<br style='mso-special-character:line-break'>
<![if !supportLineBreakNewLine]><br style='mso-special-character:line-break'>
<![endif]><o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><span style='font-family:"Courier New"'>Section 3.
Compensation.<o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><span style='font-family:"Courier New"'><o:p>&nbsp;</o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><span style='font-family:"Courier New"'>Compensation
includes direct and indirect remuneration as well as gifts or favors that are
not insubstantial.<span style='mso-spacerun:yes'>  </span>A financial interest
is not necessarily a conflict of interest.<span style='mso-spacerun:yes'> 
</span>Under Article III, Section 2, a person who has a financial interest may
have a conflict of interest only if the appropriate governing board or
committee decides that a conflict of interest exists.<o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><span style='font-family:"Courier New"'><o:p>&nbsp;</o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='text-align:center'><span
style='font-family:"Courier New"'>Article III - Procedures<o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><span style='font-family:"Courier New"'><o:p>&nbsp;</o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><span style='font-family:"Courier New"'>Section 1. Duty to
Disclose.<o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><span style='font-family:"Courier New"'><o:p>&nbsp;</o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><span style='font-family:"Courier New"'>In connection with any
actual or possible conflict of interest, an interested person must disclose the
existence of the financial interest and be given the opportunity to disclose
all material facts to the directors and members of committees with governing
board delegated powers considering the proposed transaction or arrangement<o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><span style='font-family:"Courier New"'><o:p>&nbsp;</o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><span style='font-family:"Courier New"'>Section 2.<span
style='mso-spacerun:yes'>  </span>Determining Whether a Conflict of Interest
Exists.<o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><span style='font-family:"Courier New"'><o:p>&nbsp;</o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><span style='font-family:"Courier New"'>After disclosure of
the financial interest and all material facts, and after any discussion with
the interested person, he/she shall leave the governing board or committee
meeting while the determination of a conflict of interest is discussed and
voted upon.<span style='mso-spacerun:yes'>  </span>The remaining board or
committee members shall decide if a conflict of interest exists.<o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><span style='font-family:"Courier New"'><o:p>&nbsp;</o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><span style='font-family:"Courier New"'>Section 3. Procedures
for Addressing the Conflict of Interest<o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><span style='font-family:"Courier New"'><o:p>&nbsp;</o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><span style='font-family:"Courier New"'>An interested person
may make a presentation at the governing board or committee meeting, but after
the presentation, he/she shall leave the meeting during the discussion of, and
the vote on, the transaction or arrangement involving the possible conflict of
interest.<br style='mso-special-character:line-break'>
<![if !supportLineBreakNewLine]><br style='mso-special-character:line-break'>
<![endif]><o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><span style='font-family:"Courier New"'>The chairperson of
the governing board or committee shall, if appropriate, appoint a disinterested
person or committee to investigate alternatives to the proposed transaction or
arrangement.<br style='mso-special-character:line-break'>
<![if !supportLineBreakNewLine]><br style='mso-special-character:line-break'>
<![endif]><o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><span style='font-family:"Courier New"'>After exercising due
diligence, the governing board or committee shall determine whether the
corporation can obtain with reasonable efforts a more advantageous transaction
or arrangement from a person or entity that would not give rise to a conflict
of interest.<br style='mso-special-character:line-break'>
<![if !supportLineBreakNewLine]><br style='mso-special-character:line-break'>
<![endif]><o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><span style='font-family:"Courier New"'>If a more
advantageous transaction or arrangement is not reasonably possible under
circumstances not producing a conflict of interest, the governing board or
committee shall determine by a majority vote of the disinterested directors
whether the transaction or arrangement is in the corporation’s best interest,
for its own benefit, and whether it is fair and reasonable. In conformity with
the above determination it shall make its decision as to whether to enter into
the transaction or arrangement.<br style='mso-special-character:line-break'>
<![if !supportLineBreakNewLine]><br style='mso-special-character:line-break'>
<![endif]><o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><span style='font-family:"Courier New"'>Section 4. Violations
of the Conflicts of Interest Policy.<o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><span style='font-family:"Courier New"'><o:p>&nbsp;</o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><span style='font-family:"Courier New"'>If the governing
board or committee has reasonable cause to believe a member has failed to
disclose actual or possible conflicts of interest, it shall inform the member
of the basis for such belief and afford the member an opportunity to explain
the alleged failure to disclose.<o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><span style='font-family:"Courier New"'><o:p>&nbsp;</o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><span style='font-family:"Courier New"'>If, after hearing
the member’s response and after making further investigation as warranted by
the circumstances, the governing board or committee determines the member has
failed to disclose an actual or possible conflict of interest, it shall take
appropriate disciplinary and corrective action.<o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><span style='font-family:"Courier New"'><o:p>&nbsp;</o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='text-align:center'><span
style='font-family:"Courier New"'>Article IV – Records of Proceedings<o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><span style='font-family:"Courier New"'><o:p>&nbsp;</o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><span style='font-family:"Courier New"'>The minutes of the
governing board and all committees with board delegated powers shall contain:<br
style='mso-special-character:line-break'>
<![if !supportLineBreakNewLine]><br style='mso-special-character:line-break'>
<![endif]><o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoListParagraph style='text-indent:-.25in;mso-list:l0 level1 lfo7'><![if !supportLists]><span
style='font-family:"Courier New";mso-fareast-font-family:"Courier New"'><span
style='mso-list:Ignore'>a)<span style='font:7.0pt "Times New Roman"'> </span></span></span><![endif]><span
style='font-family:"Courier New"'>The names of the persons who disclosed or
otherwise were found to have a financial interest in connection with an actual
or possible conflict of interest, the nature of the financial interest, any
action taken to determine whether a conflict of interest was present, and the
governing board’s or committee’s decision as to whether a conflict of interest
in fact existed.<o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><span style='font-family:"Courier New"'><o:p>&nbsp;</o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoListParagraph style='text-indent:-.25in;mso-list:l0 level1 lfo7'><![if !supportLists]><span
style='font-family:"Courier New";mso-fareast-font-family:"Courier New"'><span
style='mso-list:Ignore'>b)<span style='font:7.0pt "Times New Roman"'> </span></span></span><![endif]><span
style='font-family:"Courier New"'>The names of the persons who were present for
discussions and votes relating to the transaction or arrangement, the content
of the discussion, including any alternatives to the proposed transaction or
arrangement, and a record of any votes taken in connection with the
proceedings.<o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><span style='font-family:"Courier New"'><o:p>&nbsp;</o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='text-align:center'><span
style='font-family:"Courier New"'>Article V - Compensation<o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><span style='font-family:"Courier New"'><o:p>&nbsp;</o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><span style='font-family:"Courier New"'>A voting member of
the governing board who receives compensation, directly or indirectly, from the
corporation for services is precluded from voting on matters pertaining to that
member’s compensation.<o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><span style='font-family:"Courier New"'><o:p>&nbsp;</o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><span style='font-family:"Courier New"'>A voting member of
any committee whose jurisdiction includes compensation matters and who receives
compensation, directly or indirectly, from the corporation for services is
precluded from voting on matters pertaining to that member’s compensation.<o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><span style='font-family:"Courier New"'><o:p>&nbsp;</o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><span style='font-family:"Courier New"'>No voting member of
the governing board or any committee whose jurisdiction includes compensation
matters and who receives compensation, directly or indirectly, from the
corporation, either individually or collectively, is prohibited from providing
information to any committee regarding compensation.<o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><span style='font-family:"Courier New"'><o:p>&nbsp;</o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='page-break-after:avoid'><span style='font-family:

<p class=MsoNormal style='page-break-after:avoid'><span style='font-family:

<![if !supportLineBreakNewLine]><br style='mso-special-character:line-break'>
<![endif]><o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoListParagraphCxSpFirst style='text-indent:-.25in;mso-list:l4 level1 lfo5'><![if !supportLists]><span

<p class=MsoNormal><span style='font-family:"Courier New"'><o:p>&nbsp;</o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='text-align:center'><span
style='font-family:"Courier New"'>Article VII – Periodic Reviews<o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><span style='font-family:"Courier New"'><o:p>&nbsp;</o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><span style='font-family:"Courier New"'>To ensure the
corporation operates in a manner consistent with charitable purposes and does
not engage in activities that could jeopardize its tax-exempt status, periodic
reviews shall be conducted.<span style='mso-spacerun:yes'>  </span>The periodic
reviews shall, at a minimum, include the following subjects:<br
style='mso-special-character:line-break'>
<![if !supportLineBreakNewLine]><br style='mso-special-character:line-break'>
<![endif]><o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoListParagraphCxSpFirst style='text-indent:-.25in;mso-list:l6 level1 lfo11'><![if !supportLists]><span
style='font-family:"Courier New";mso-fareast-font-family:"Courier New"'><span
style='mso-list:Ignore'>a)<span style='font:7.0pt "Times New Roman"'> </span></span></span><![endif]><span
style='font-family:"Courier New"'>Whether compensation arrangements and benefits
are reasonable, based on competent survey information and the result of arm’s
length bargaining.<br style='mso-special-character:line-break'>
<![if !supportLineBreakNewLine]><br style='mso-special-character:line-break'>
<![endif]><o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoListParagraphCxSpLast style='text-indent:-.25in;mso-list:l6 level1 lfo11'><![if !supportLists]><span

style='font-family:"Courier New"'>Whether partnerships, joint ventures, and
arrangements with management corporations conform to the corporation’s written
policies, are properly recorded, reflect reasonable investment or payments for
goods and services, further charitable purposes and do not result in inurement,
impermissible private benefit or in an excess benefit transaction.<o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><span style='font-family:"Courier New"'><o:p>&nbsp;</o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='text-align:center'><span
style='font-family:"Courier New"'>Article VIII – Use of Outside Experts<o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><span style='font-family:"Courier New"'><o:p>&nbsp;</o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><span style='font-family:"Courier New"'>When conducting the
periodic reviews as provided for in Article VII, the corporation may, but need
